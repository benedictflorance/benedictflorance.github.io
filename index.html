Biography

I am an undergrad with a B. Tech. (Honors) in Computer Science and Engineering from NIT Trichy, one of the top ten engineering schools of India. My areas of interest are machine learning, deep learning and computer vision. I am currently working on unsupervised 3D human pose estimation with Prof. Venkatesh Babu at the VAL Lab, IISc Bangalore. I have been fortunate to work with amazing mentors, Prof. Eric Granger (LIVIA lab, ETS Montreal, Canada), Prof. C. V. Jawahar(CVIT lab, IIIT Hyderabad) and Prof. Leela Velusamy (NIT Trichy), on diverse areas of computer vision including domain adaptation, human pose estimation, object detection and applications in sports, medicine and biodiversity. I am excited to wield the technical expertise that I have gained to teach computers to see, pixel by pixel.

I was an active member of the web-team and machine learning group of Spider, the R&D Club of NIT Trichy](https://spider.nitt.edu). I am also a recipient of several prestigious awards like the Vector Scholarship in Artificial Intelligence, Mitacs Globalink Research Internship Award and Indian Academy of Sciences’ Summer Research Fellowship. Apart from academics, I love to organize events, lead and work as a team with a diverse set of people. I led the Workshops and Informals Team of NIT Trichy’s Festember, South India’s largest student-run cultural festival and chaired the Rolling Reels Film Festival. In my leisure time, I avidly follow current affairs and teach school children from marginalized communities through Illuminate.
