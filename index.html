Biography

I am a senior undergrad pursuing B. Tech. (Honors) in Computer Science and Engineering from NIT Trichy, one of the top ten engineering schools of India. My areas of interest are machine learning, deep learning and computer vision. I am currently working on “Unsupervised Deep Domain Adaptation for Object Detection” with Prof. Eric Granger at the LIVIA lab, ETS Montreal, Canada. In 2018, I worked with Prof C. V. Jawahar at the CVIT lab, IIIT Hyderabad on Indian Flora Project, a plant species identification and disease detection system. I am also a recipient of the Mitacs Globalink Research Internship Award, Indian Academy of Sciences’ Summer Research Fellowship and Google India Scholarship for Web Specialist.

I am a computer vision researcher and web developer at Spider, the R&D Club of NIT Trichy. I have participated in several hackathons, contributed to open-source and web-development projects both in the academia as well as the industry. I love to organize events, lead and work as a team with a diverse set of people. I am heading the Workshops and Informals Team of NIT Trichy’s Festember, South India’s largest student-run cultural festival, leading a team of 80 members to organize workshops and informal events during Festember. I have held multiple positions of responsibilities in various teams ranging from technical to leadership roles. I spend my leisure time by reading and writing articles and appreciating good music. I also care for educational and social rights. I am a part of Illuminate, a government-certified charitable trust that aims to provide a wholesome education to children from marginalized communities.
